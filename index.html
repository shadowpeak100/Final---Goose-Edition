A story from a victim

It all started on my mission. There I was, eating a delicious 5 peso waffle in the Philippines when suddenly a goose came up and started hissing at me. What else hisses you may ask? The answer is snakes and snakes are bad. The hissing continued and I was not impressed by this show of aggression. Like the peaceful missionary I was, I put up my umbrella between the goose and me. I couldn't leave and I had done nothing wrong. Little did I know this was the only action needed for war. The goose came around, looked at me and then bit my leg. Needless to say I was not impressed. It was like throwing a rubber duck at Hercules and thats approximately what it instigated. I shooed the satanic honking abomination away but that vile creature did not relent. Little did it know, it brought a beak to a rubber shoe fight. I had worn the right shoes that day, rubbery crocs that can bounce anything away and I had been practicing my kicking abilities, believing I was the karate kid. After all, I was in Asia. The goose continued to bite me, my fuse burned short then it got what it had so eagerly bartered for. A square kick send it tumbling into the field. Not enough to injure it, after all it was 0.001 percent of my kicking power. It turned and I started walking towards it again, suddenly logic clicked and it started to leave. An important lesson was taught that day: there are no rewards, there are no punishments; there are only consequences. Even now, I think of my important role that day, as a teacher of fortune cookie-like wisdom.
